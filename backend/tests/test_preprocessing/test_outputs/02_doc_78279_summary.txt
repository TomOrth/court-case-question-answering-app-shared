Title: Complaint for Injunctive and Declaratory Relief and Monetary Damages
Date: 2009-10-22

================================================================================

The document is a complaint filed on October 22, 2009, in the United States District Court for the District of Idaho, by Plaintiffs Janet F. Bell, Brian S. Carson, Craig Fox, Robert Martin, Lawrence Lee Smith, Robert Anderson, and Pamela S. Hawkes against the City of Boise, the Boise Police Department, and Chief of Police Michael Masterson. The Plaintiffs seek injunctive and declaratory relief, as well as monetary damages, alleging violations of their constitutional rights under the Eighth and Fourteenth Amendments, and corresponding provisions of the Idaho Constitution, due to the enforcement of certain municipal codes that criminalize homelessness [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00000].

The complaint outlines that Boise has a significant homeless population, estimated between 2,000 and 4,500 individuals on any given night, while local shelters can accommodate only about 300 people, with additional overflow space for approximately 400 more [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00001]. The Plaintiffs assert that they have been cited and arrested under Boise Municipal Code § 9-10-02, which prohibits camping in public places, and § 6-01-05(A), which prohibits sleeping in public as disorderly conduct. They argue that these laws effectively criminalize homelessness and violate their constitutional rights, as they have no choice but to sleep in public due to the lack of available shelter [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00002].

Each Plaintiff provides personal accounts of their experiences with law enforcement and the impact of these citations on their lives. For example, Janet F. Bell has received multiple citations for sleeping in public, which has led to a suspended sentence and fear of incarceration if cited again [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00003]. Brian S. Carson describes being cited for sleeping next to a building after being turned away from shelters, resulting in jail time and financial burdens [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00002]. Craig Fox, who suffers from arthritis, was cited for sleeping on a park bench while waiting for a shelter to open [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00002]. Robert Martin has faced multiple citations despite his disabilities and inability to find shelter [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00002]. Lawrence Lee Smith and Robert Anderson also recount similar experiences of being cited for sleeping in public areas due to the lack of shelter options [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00002].

The complaint further discusses the broader implications of the enforcement of these ordinances, asserting that they exacerbate the cycle of homelessness by making it difficult for individuals to secure employment and housing due to criminal records stemming from these citations [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00005]. The Plaintiffs argue that the enforcement practices of the Boise Police Department are part of a policy to drive homeless individuals out of the city, which they claim violates their rights to travel and due process [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00006].

The Plaintiffs seek a range of remedies, including a temporary restraining order against the enforcement of the cited municipal codes, expungement of records for those unlawfully cited, reimbursement for fines and costs incurred, and a declaration that the enforcement actions violate their constitutional rights [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00008]. They also request a jury trial [CITE:doc_78279_chunk_00008].

In summary, this complaint highlights the struggles of homeless individuals in Boise, the legal challenges they face due to municipal ordinances, and the constitutional implications of criminalizing homelessness. The Plaintiffs argue that the enforcement of these laws not only violates their rights but also fails to address the underlying issues of homelessness in the city.
