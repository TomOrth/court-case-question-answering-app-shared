The document is a Settlement Agreement Term Sheet dated February 8, 2021, related to the case Martin et al v. City of Boise, case number 1:09-cv-00540-REB. The parties involved are the Plaintiffs, Robert Martin, Pamela Hawkes, and Robert Anderson, and the Defendant, the City of Boise [CITE:doc_154974_chunk_00000].

The Term Sheet outlines the essential terms of the final settlement between the Parties. Upon signing the Term Sheet, the Parties agree to a full and final resolution of all claims in the case. The Settlement Agreement, which is attached as Exhibit B, contains the binding terms of the settlement and is enforceable to the fullest extent of the law [CITE:doc_154974_chunk_00000].

Key terms of the settlement include the amendment of certain sections of the Boise City Code: Section 7-3A-2, known as the "Camping Ordinance," and Section 5-2-3, referred to as the "Disorderly Conduct Ordinance." The amendments to these ordinances are included in the Term Sheet as Exhibits C and D, respectively [CITE:doc_154974_chunk_00000].

The Parties have agreed to seek a dismissal of the case by joint stipulation, which is contingent upon several conditions: 
1. Approval of the Settlement Agreement terms by the City of Boise.
2. Appropriation of funds by the City of Boise as detailed in the Settlement Agreement.
3. Amendment of the Camping and Disorderly Conduct Ordinances to align with Exhibits C and D [CITE:doc_154974_chunk_00000].

Once these conditions, referred to as "Conditional Terms," are met, the Parties will execute a full and final release of all claims, including any claims for damages, fees, and costs. The completion of other terms in the Settlement Agreement is not required prior to this release, although they remain binding obligations [CITE:doc_154974_chunk_00000].

Additionally, the Parties have agreed on the payment of Boise Homeless Initiative funds, damages, and attorneys' fees and costs as specified in the Settlement Agreement [CITE:doc_154974_chunk_00000].
