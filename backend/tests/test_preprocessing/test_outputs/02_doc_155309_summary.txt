The document titled "Exhibit B - Settlement Agreement" dated February 8, 2021, outlines the terms of a settlement in the case Martin v. Boise, aimed at protecting the constitutional rights of homeless individuals in Boise, Idaho. The settlement seeks to prevent the issuance of citations or incarceration for sleeping, sitting, lying down, or camping outdoors on city or public property when no alternative shelter is available [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000].

Key principles guiding the settlement include:
- Under the Eighth Amendment, homeless individuals cannot be penalized for sleeping outdoors when there is no available shelter [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000].
- The determination of available shelter space considers both the existence of available sleeping spaces and the individual's personal circumstances [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000].
- The City commits to not imposing criminal penalties for camping outdoors when no shelter is available, while also recognizing that some individuals may have conditions preventing them from utilizing available shelters [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000].
- The City will enforce ordinances against individuals only when there is available shelter space for them [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000].
- The City aims to explore solutions to reduce homelessness, including increasing affordable housing availability [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000].

The agreement stipulates that the City will implement mandatory annual training for police officers on engaging with homeless individuals and enforcing relevant ordinances, with input from the plaintiffs on training materials [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000].

Additionally, the City agrees to adopt guidance in the Boise Police Department’s Policy Manual regarding ordinance enforcement. Officers are required to assess available overnight shelter based on individual circumstances before determining if camping or disorderly conduct has occurred [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000]. Officers must inform homeless individuals about available shelter, and if they decline, a written warning may be issued [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000].

The City established a Homeless Outreach Unit in February 2020, consisting of three full-time employees dedicated to assisting the homeless community with access to resources and services [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000]. 

In terms of compliance and recordkeeping, the City will provide the plaintiffs with a written status report within 30 days of executing the settlement and bi-annual records of citations issued under relevant ordinances for the first year [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000]. The City agrees to cooperate with requests for public records related to these citations [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00000].

The agreement includes a Coronavirus 2021 Addendum acknowledging CDC guidance on risks associated with close-quarters contact among homeless individuals during the COVID-19 pandemic, recognizing that individual health circumstances may affect shelter availability [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00001].

Financially, the City will allocate $1,335,000 in general funds for services to the homeless, with at least 33% designated for temporary overnight shelters to expand shelter infrastructure [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00001]. The City will also pay $5,000 in damages to the plaintiffs and $435,000 in attorneys’ fees [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00001].

Upon finalization of the settlement, the plaintiffs will dismiss their action with prejudice, contingent upon the Boise City Council's approval of the settlement terms and the appropriation of funds [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00001]. The settlement will operate as a general release of the City from all remaining claims, with each party bearing its own attorney’s fees and costs, except as specified [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00001]. The parties also agree that the settlement will not be confidential [CITE:doc_155309_chunk_00001].
