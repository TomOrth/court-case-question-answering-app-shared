Title: Revised Second Amended Complaint for Injunctive and Declaratory Relief
Date: 2014-07-31

================================================================================

The Revised Second Amended Complaint for Injunctive and Declaratory Relief was filed on July 31, 2014, in the United States District Court for the District of Idaho, under Case No. 1:09-CV-540-REB. The plaintiffs, Robert Martin, Lawrence Lee Smith, Robert Anderson, Basil E. Humphrey, Pamela S. Hawkes, and Janet F. Bell, are individuals who are homeless and are suing the City of Boise, the Boise Police Department, and Chief of Police Michael Masterson in his official capacity for violations of their constitutional rights, specifically under the Eighth Amendment [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00000].

The complaint outlines that there is a significant homelessness issue in Boise, with thousands of individuals lacking shelter on any given night. The local shelters can only accommodate approximately 460 people, leading many to sleep in public spaces, which is in violation of Boise Municipal Code ordinances that prohibit "camping" and "disorderly conduct" [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00000]. The plaintiffs assert that these laws effectively criminalize homelessness, as they are enforced against individuals who have no choice but to sleep in public due to the lack of available shelter [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00000].

The plaintiffs have faced repeated citations and arrests for sleeping in public, which has resulted in convictions that further complicate their ability to secure employment and housing. For example, Robert Martin has been cited multiple times for sleeping outside shelters when he was unable to find accommodation [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00001]. Lawrence Lee Smith has also faced similar issues, receiving citations for sleeping in secluded areas and experiencing incarceration as a result [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00001]. The complaint details the experiences of each plaintiff, illustrating the cycle of homelessness exacerbated by the enforcement of these ordinances [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00001].

The complaint argues that the enforcement of Boise Municipal Code §§ 9-10-02 and 6-01-05(A) constitutes cruel and unusual punishment under the Eighth Amendment, as it punishes individuals for their status as homeless persons [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00002]. The plaintiffs seek declaratory and injunctive relief, requesting that the court enjoin the defendants from enforcing these ordinances against individuals sleeping or lying down in public and declare the ordinances unconstitutional [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00002].

Jurisdiction is established under 28 U.S.C. §§ 1331, 1343(a)(3) and (4), and 1367, as the plaintiffs aim to vindicate rights established by the Eighth Amendment [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00003]. The complaint also discusses the inadequacies of the shelter system in Boise, noting that many shelters have restrictive policies that prevent certain individuals from accessing services, thereby increasing the number of people who are forced to sleep in public [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00006].

In summary, the plaintiffs allege that the City of Boise and its police department have a policy and practice of unlawfully enforcing ordinances that criminalize homelessness, which leads to further marginalization and suffering for the homeless population [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00004]. They seek relief from the court to address these violations and to prevent further enforcement of the cited ordinances against homeless individuals [CITE:doc_78327_chunk_00007].
