Title: Joint Motion for Dismissal with Prejudice
Date: 2021-02-08
Chunks: 1

================================================================================

On February 8, 2021, Plaintiffs Robert Martin, Pamela Hawkes, and Robert Anderson filed a Joint Motion for Dismissal with Prejudice against the Defendant City of Boise in the United States District Court for the District of Idaho, case number 1:09-cv-0540-REB [CITE:doc_154973_chunk_00000]. The motion was made under Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 41(a) and indicated that the Parties had reached a settlement regarding the claims in the action, as detailed in the attached Settlement Agreement Term Sheet (Exhibit A) and the Settlement Agreement (Exhibit B).

The motion stipulates that the dismissal of all claims is contingent upon several conditions: (1) approval of the Settlement Agreement terms by the City of Boise, (2) appropriation of funds as outlined in the Settlement Agreement, and (3) amendments to the Camping and Disorderly Conduct Ordinances (Exhibits C and D) as agreed upon by the Parties. If these conditions are met, the Court's dismissal will serve as a release of the City and its agents, officers, and employees from all remaining claims in the case [CITE:doc_154973_chunk_00001].

Additionally, the motion states that each party will bear its own costs, expenses, and attorneys’ fees, unless otherwise specified in the Settlement Agreement [CITE:doc_154973_chunk_00002]. The Parties committed to notifying the Court once the conditions of the settlement are fulfilled [CITE:doc_154973_chunk_00003].

The motion was signed by attorneys representing both the Plaintiffs and the Defendant, including Howard A. Belodoff from Idaho Legal Aid Services, Michael E. Bern, Allen M. Gardner, Danielle McCall, and Gemma Donofrio from Latham & Watkins LLP, as well as Eric Tars from the National Homelessness Law Center for the Plaintiffs, and Jayme Sullivan and Scott Muir from the Boise City Attorney’s Office for the Defendant [CITE:doc_154973_chunk_00004].

The document also included a Certificate of Service confirming that the motion was electronically filed and served to relevant parties on the same day [CITE:doc_154973_chunk_00005].
